Help! A friend is suicidal!

If you don't think they are actively making an attempt, dial 811 and talk to the suicide hotline. If you feel they are in immediate danger to themselves, dial 911. The best thing to do is keep them talking to you. Ask them things about themselves, have them focus on their surroundings, and things of that nature. If they stop responding after 5 minutes, dial 911 and inform the operator of the situation.

Help! My friend made a suicide attempt!

Dial 911 right away. If they are bleeding, wrap a shirt or other cloth tightly around the wound - the more sterile the cloth the better. Don't use a shirt you've worn for the day as it risks infection, but rather a clean piece of cloth. Your shirt should be the last resort. If they have overdosed, lie them on their side and loosen any tight clothing. If they are on a high building or bridge, call 911 right away, as someone experienced in suicide prevention will need to assist. Remember: Always keep them talking.

I just need coping skills.

Coping skills are a great resource for many people. The same ones aren't always effective, but coping skills work for everyone. No two people will always have the same coping skills! Below is a list of a few popular ones - but any activity can be seen as a coping skill! What's important is that it calms you down!

  • Sports/Athletics/Working out
  • Walking/Jogging/Running
  • Reading/Writing
  • Painting/Drawing/Sketching/Doodling
  • Mechanics/Coding/Engineering
  • Talking/Venting
  • Bathing/Cold Shower
  • Listening to/Making Music
  • Watch TV/YouTube/TikTok
  • Cleaning your environment
  • Writing jokes and reading them aloud
  • Go outside/Meditate
